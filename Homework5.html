injected14 = table.query(f'13.8 < Signal < 14.2')
plt.hist(injected14['Data'], bins =100, density = True, color='green')
plt.xlabel('Observed Signal')
plt.ylabel('Probability')
plt.title('True Signal = 14 | Observed Signal Spread')
plt.show()

This plot explains the distribution of the data (which encompassing everything such as background noise) after injecting a true signal of 14. Image this plot is a slice horizontally of the 2D histogram plotted at the very beginning with 14 being the true signal value (y-axis). Looking at Bayes' Theorem, we can see that with the normalization factor of P(data), which the background distribution is normal, therefore P(data|signal) is proportional to P(signal|data). That means they will have the same graph if they are normalized.
